Gradients & (De)Gradations: A Visual Study of Art & Computation

Calvin Deutschbein

“AI Art” has been an emergent trend since capital investment in cryptocurrencies in early 2022 drove marketing around NFTs, which frequently were associated with generative art. The emergence of large language models (LLMs) such as ChatGPT in November 2022 drove this trend further to temporary virality, but the popularity of the search term has remained relatively durable for the last two years.

To me there is a core problem: I feel “AI Art” doesn’t exist. I believe that art is produced by an artist, and that artists are formed by unique living experiences, including the cultivation of an artistic practice, and absent the process of being produced by an artist, as work of visual media (or any other medium) may be something that looks like art, but is fundamentally distinct in an important way.

Through a study of visual media, I present and support my hypothesis that art is produced by artists, not by the combination of technologies regarded as “AI”. As a supporting claim, I will note that I regard “AI” technologies as a much closer analog to paints and paintbrushes, to clay and bronze, or to lighting and film - an exciting and valuable tool that can facilitate novel approaches to artistic expression, but a tool that is completely neutral with regard to the core artistic content of a produced work.

Ex1 The End of Data (Yoko Taro et al)

Yoko Taro is the artistic director of the Drakengard and Nier series of media, which includes interactive, anime, and orchestral productions. Within this series The End of Data is a promotional work for the orchestral concert series of the same name, and to me represents the sharpest visual expression of the themes present within the series.

The End of Data depicts two figures overlooking a vast ruinous expanse, evocative of Shelley’s Ozymandias or Friedrich’s Wanderer above the Sea of Fog. It is a work in constant dialogue with other art and artists. It makes me feel at once alone and seen - two figures, with another yet separate from all else, and a stand-alone work of artistry part of a rich tapestry of expressions grasping at what it means to think and feel. I know that I want to feel, but that I don’t even know what it means to do so.

Ex2 Vengeful Spirit (Eddy ‘Eddy Gonz’ González Dávila)

Vengeful Spirit depicts the inflection point of the sprawling artistic setting Warhammer 40.000 (‘40K`), a post-parody reflection of the white supremacist-capitalist-patriachy’s imperial decline. I appreciate ‘40K’ as a child of the internet and post-Cold War era, because I believe this setting depicts the mythos of the European project more appropriately than prior mythology like the matière de Bretagne or the Tolkien Legendarium. To me, Eddy Gonz captures the core contribution of each of these myths as the Hegelian notion of contradiction: empire contradicts itself through the chaos they must create via permanent expansion, and the order they must impose on the interior - both unnatural. Vengeful Spirit depicts the explosive resolution of these contradictions through avatars of chaos and order. It makes me feel grounded that even the most adroit propaganda will necessarily depict empire as violent and impossible.

Ex3 island200.500.1000bb (Melinda “Superliminal” Green)

In 1993, Melinda Green discovered what she called the “Buddhabrot” technique for rendering the Mandelbrot fractal. The Mandelbrot is a set - a subset of the complex number plane in which numbers are included in a binary fashion. Green’s insight was that this binary rendering of sets was, in brief, artistically uninteresting. Instead, Green found ordered collections of numbers that tended toward infinite, but transiently passed through the set, and colored the complex plane based on the frequency that numbers occurred within these series.

To me, this represents the beauty inherent in mathematics and visual media that emerges when disregarding binary classifications.

Ex4 ∅ (Jonatas “igooverland” Neiva)

@igooverland spent 5 hours of timelapse nighttime digital photography atop the highest elevation pass within the contiguous interstate highway system of the “united states”. This work to me represents the one-sided dialogue between the best efforts of the colonizer and the vast expanse of the uncolonized universe, and leaves me with a profound sense of hope and inspiration for a gentler future.

The eisenhower interstate highway system, to my mind, represents the twin hands of empire: “land dispossession” as an industrial build-out of western expansion of the united states, and “forced migration” as a component of the united states security apparatus, which invested strongly in the system during Cold War to “free up” additional troops for military actions in primarily Korea and Vietnam, two nations whose late 20th century history is characterized by forced migration through us-imposed national partitions. To see the limits of imperialism cast as so small and irrelevant, even at the height of their power, warms my heart.
